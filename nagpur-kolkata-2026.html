01 The Story

Two Indian Observances, One Calling

In the closing days of January and the opening days of February 2026, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID moved through two of India's most spiritually significant observances. On the Republic Day of India, in central Nagpur, J AARON K DAVID was named Chief Guest of the Alms Offering Ceremony and the 151 Buddha Statue Distribution at the Buddha Spiritual Park. Five days later, in Kolkata, both SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID were honoured as Special Guests of the Walking-PEACE · Symbolic Peace Talk and Peace March for Maghi Purnima, marking 75 Years of Peace at the Tollygunge Sambodhi Buddhist Monastery.

The two observances are not unrelated. Republic Day commemorates the founding charter of modern India; Maghi Purnima commemorates the Buddha's announcement of the timing of his parinibbāna. Both are observances of national and spiritual covenant — and the recognitions conferred on SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID at both placed them inside the trusted civic and dharmic register of their Indian hosts.

"SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP from Malaysia · Special Guest. J AARON K DAVID, Co-Founder and Partner of Crowned Eagles Global LLP · Special Guest. Walk for Peace & Peace Day Observation, Kolkata, February 1, 2026."

02 Nagpur · Republic Day · 26 January 2026

From the Airport Garland to the Chief Guest Pulpit

SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID arrived at Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport, Nagpur on the morning of 26 January 2026 — India's Republic Day. The organising society greeted them at the arrival gate with flower garlands — the customary Indian welcome reserved for honoured guests of a public observance. From the airport the delegation drove directly to the Buddha Spiritual Park, Sihora-Kanhan, where the day's programme — the Alms Offering Ceremony and the 151 Buddha Statue Distribution — was already convening under the printed posters bearing each founder's name.

The Buddha Spiritual Park, Sihora, Kanhan · Nagpur, India

On the morning of India's Republic Day, the official programme of the Alms Offering Ceremony and 151 Buddha Statue Distribution named both SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP — Founder and Managing Partner of Crowned Eagles Global LLP and CE Global Network LLP — and J AARON K DAVID — Co-Founder and Partner — as Chief Guests of the ceremony, each honoured on their own official poster issued by the organising society.

The role of Chief Guest at an Indian Buddhist observance of this scale is not honorary in name only; it carries the responsibility of opening the ceremonial Alms Offering and symbolically commissioning the distribution of the 151 Buddha statues to the gathered monastic and lay community. SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID shared the programme with Bhante Vinacharya (Delhi), Bollywood's Himanshu Soni (best known for portraying the Buddha in Indian television), and Nitin Gajbhiye, President of the Ahilyabai Holkar Multipurpose Education Society.

02b On the Ground · Tathagat Buddha Vihar · Hiwari Nagar

A Humanitarian Mission, Witnessed and Photographed

Following the morning ceremony at the Buddha Spiritual Park, the day's mission moved to Tathagat Buddha Vihar, Hiwari Nagar, Nagpur for the evening service — a public food and blanket distribution under the Crowned Eagles Global banner, jointly held with the Ahilyabai Holkar Multipurpose Education Society. The photographs below document the official welcome banner, the founders received at the venue, and the formal alms-offering of robes and blankets to the resident monastics before the larger public distribution began.

A Sequence Recorded by Witnesses

"GOD OF THE UNIVERSE Bless You, Heal You, Restore You…"

The banner at the entrance of Tathagat Buddha Vihar opened the evening service with the printed blessing — "GOD OF THE UNIVERSE bless you, heal you, restore you with the warmth of their love and give you hope." It named SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP as Founder and Managing Partner and J AARON K DAVID as Co-Founder and Partner of Crowned Eagles Global LLP and CE Global Network LLP, and dedicated the food-and-blanket distribution to the poor, the needy, the hungry, and the unwell in all areas of their lives.

Inside the vihar, beneath the seated Buddha and the gathered villagers, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID each personally offered folded monastic robes to the resident monks — a ceremonial alms-offering witnessed by hundreds and photographed for the public record. The Indian, Malaysian and Singaporean flags printed on the banner alongside the Crowned Eagles Global crest marked the mission's tri-national character: Singapore-registered, India-served, regionally supported.

03 Kolkata · Maghi Purnima · 1 February 2026

Special Guests at the Walking-PEACE Observance

Maghi Purnima — the full moon of the holy Magha month — is one of the most significant days in the Indian Buddhist calendar. On this morning in 2026, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID walked at the head of the Walking-PEACE procession in Regent Park, Kolkata, beside the senior monastics of the Tollygunge Moore Avenue Buddha Samity — the historic Buddhist monastery established in 1949.

The theme of the observance — "Ahimsa · Karuna · Coexistence — Compassion Knows No Boundaries" — placed SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID at the symbolic head of an international peace procession that linked Kolkata to global peace walks announced under the same UNPKFC framework.

Beyond the monastery, the Kolkata visit also brought SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID to Sister Nivedita University (SNU) in Newtown — first for a university award conferment, then on a separate day for a far more personal moment: a funeral service in which both founders were asked to lead the worship.

Sister Nivedita University — named for the Irish-born disciple of Swami Vivekananda who served the poor of Kolkata until her death in 1911 — conferred a university award upon SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID in the presence of senior monastic and university dignitaries, including the venerable Bhante and the chancellor's office.

A Separate Day · The Funeral Service

"LORD, Make Me to Know My End"

Sister Nivedita University · Kolkata · A separate day in the same visit

On another day during the same Kolkata visit, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID were invited back to Sister Nivedita University — this time not for an honour, but to participate in a funeral service being held within the university community. They were asked to lead the worship.

The song they led was drawn from Psalm 39: "LORD, make me to know mine end, and the measure of my days, what it is; that I may know how frail I am." A psalm of David — chosen for its quiet honesty about mortality and the brevity of the human life — sung over a grieving community by two visitors from Singapore who had been received with such honour earlier in the same week, and who now stood before the bereaved to offer a moment of comfort.

The trust extended by the university — to invite outside visitors into a funeral service and ask them to carry the worship — is not a small thing. It is the kind of recognition that does not appear on a plaque or a certificate, but speaks to the way SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID are received by the communities they visit: not as celebrities to be photographed with, but as people who can be trusted to carry weight when weight needs to be carried.
