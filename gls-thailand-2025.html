01 International Recognition

Received Among the World's Peace Leaders

In December 2025, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID were received in Bangkok, Thailand, at the Global Leadership Summit 2025 (GLS 2025) — the annual flagship convening of the United Peace Keepers Federal Council (UNPKFC), an international body that gathers peace leaders, peacekeepers, humanitarian advocates, and dignitaries from more than 160 countries and global organisations.

The Summit that welcomed SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID is convened each year under the leadership of Dr. Aphinita Chaichana, Founder and Global President of UNPKFC — a body whose previous editions (GLS 2023, GLS 2024) have welcomed international peace leaders, UN-affiliated delegations, members of the diplomatic corps, and senior representatives of partner organisations including the United Nations Association of Coachella Valley California Chapter (UNA-USA), the Himalayan Space Centre, The Royal Society of St. George (California Branch), the United Nations Association of Germany, and others. Within this peer circle, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID were received as international delegates in their own right.

To be present in this delegation places SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID within an unambiguously international circle — one whose attendance roster routinely includes peacekeeping officials, serving and retired military leaders, government representatives, and humanitarian advocates from across Asia, the Americas, Europe, and Africa.

"Their presence in Bangkok this December was a quiet endorsement of the international credibility SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID now carry — recognised within circles where peace leadership and humanitarian service are the price of admission."

02 Among Distinguished Delegates

In the Room with Peace Leaders & Peacekeepers

The official GLS 2025 delegate portrait places SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID among more than one hundred international delegates — peace officers in blue-beret service uniform, humanitarian leaders, members of the Thai veterans' associations, young peace ambassadors, and senior partners of the UNPKFC global network. The setting alone communicates the calibre of company SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID are now keeping.

03 The Calibre of Their Welcome

Personally Received by UNPKFC Leadership

Beyond the formal proceedings, SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID were personally received by the senior leadership of UNPKFC — placing them within the close, trusted layer of the Federation's international network, not merely among its registered delegates.

Distinguished Host · Faith Behind the Recognition

Dr. Aphinita Chaichana

Founder & Global President, UNPKFC  ·  Vice President for Southeast Asia, Himalayan Space Centre  ·  Global Chairman for Southeast Asia, UNA-USA (Coachella Valley Chapter)

Dr. Aphinita Chaichana is one of Asia's most internationally connected peace-leadership voices — recognised in May 2025 by the United Nations Association of Coachella Valley California Chapter (UNA-USA) as the first Thai woman to receive their Outstanding Leadership in Peace and Humanitarianism honour. To be received personally by Dr. Aphinita places SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID within the trusted core of the UNPKFC global network — a circle whose calibre is set not by entertainment or commerce, but by international diplomacy, humanitarian service, and recognition by UN-affiliated bodies.

Distinguished Host · UNPKFC Thailand

Ms. Rangsima Thongpunchung

Country Director, UNPKFC Thailand  ·  Senior Coordinator, Global Leadership Summit

Ms. Rangsima Thongpunchung serves as the Country Director of UNPKFC Thailand and as one of Dr. Aphinita Chaichana's most trusted associates in the coordination of UNPKFC's international engagements — including the joint welcoming of national peace-veterans associations into the Federation's collaborative peace-building initiatives. SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID were received in Bangkok with her direct hospitality — the kind of close, personal welcome that signals an enduring, long-form relationship with the Federation, not a one-off attendance.

The continuity matters. SHEKINAIH SIEW SIN YAP and J AARON K DAVID previously received UNPKFC's Certificate of Honor and the Distinguished Emblem for Positive Social Development — documented in 2024 in their humanitarian record. Their return to Bangkok in December 2025 is not first contact; it is the deepening of an established international relationship.
